The Avianca Incident

Our journey to Costa Rica on Avianca Airlines was going smoothly until the final thirty minutes of our first flight, when the undeniable, SpongeBob-style wafts of pure poop started rolling into the back rows. Cheyan and I looked at each other in silent horror as the smell intensified with each passing minute. We tried breathing through our mouths, burying our faces in our shirts, nothing worked. The turbulence of the odor was worse than any air pocket.

When we finally landed, we thought the nightmare was over. But fate had other plans. For our connecting flight, we were assigned the EXACT SAME PLANE. Same seats. Same smell. Same lingering poop particles recycling through the cabin air. I'm convinced that plane needed to be decommissioned, not refueled.

To this day, "Avianca" isn't an airline to us. It's a war story. And somehow, we survived it together, which honestly might be the ultimate test of a relationship.
